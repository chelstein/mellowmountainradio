The year we ran a tone off the tower

For about a year, KAZM sent a set of low tones out of the 780 AM tower — 5,000 watts — on a slow, repeating loop. Four frequencies: 54, 72, 84, and 111 Hz, five minutes each — and then a final five minutes with all four running together in coherence before the cycle began again.

We didn't hold them dead steady, though. We walked the phase by a tiny 0.01 step, so the waveform never quite repeated — it slowly drew a triangle pattern instead of a flat tone.

And because 780 AM is a night-time skywave signal, that little triangular fingerprint didn't stay in the Verde Valley. After dark it bounced off the ionosphere and rode north — far enough to be heard and logged all the way up in Alaska, a couple thousand miles from the red rocks.

The four frequencies were chosen for harmonic clarity and emotional neutrality — a clean, calm sound — not for any medical or scientific claim, then or now.
