The Listeners’ Lounge

Radio used to vanish the moment it aired — in the Lounge, the last fourteen days of KAZM are right here, block by block. The morning show you slept through, the song you half-caught in the canyon, the ballgame’s final inning. Pick a day, drop the needle anywhere. After two weeks, each block is gone for good — that’s the law of the license, and honestly, it suits radio.

🪟 Out the station window

These are the actual photographs out the station’s back door — the tower, the big dish, the rain gauge, the red dirt — and the window is alive. When the sun rides high it becomes the frame shot at true midday, that deep Sedona blue; toward evening it crossfades into the dusk frame; after dark it sinks into true night with real stars and the moon’s true phase. The beacon breathes on the real tower after dark, the moon and the clouds pass behind the real trees, and the whole yard shifts gently as you move your mouse — or tilt your phone — because a window is a place, not a picture. When it rains out there, drops cling to the glass and bend the scene upside-down inside them; on a cold still morning the glass fogs at the real dew point, and yes — you can wipe it with your finger.
