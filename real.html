Why bother?

Most station websites are decoration — a stock photo of a microphone, a “now playing” widget that’s really just a guess, a playlist that hasn’t updated since somebody left. KAZM’s isn’t built that way.

Every live number here is read from something that actually happened: a song that actually aired, a cloud that’s actually over Sedona right now, a car that’s actually stopped on 89A. If we can’t get the real thing, we’d rather show you nothing at all than fake it.
