Know before you crawl

The rules are simple and the desert is patient. It will outlast your warranty.

🚘 Stay on the route

Every legal trail here is on the Coconino’s Motor Vehicle Use Map. One set of tracks off-route scars cryptobiotic soil for decades — and carries a real fine. The rock is the playground; the dirt beside it is not.

🎫 Passes & permits

A Red Rock Pass covers parking at most trailheads (buy at kiosks or ranger stations). Fire restrictions change what’s legal out there — the live restriction card is on our Weather page, straight from the Forest Service.

📶 Cell service dies out there

Broken Arrow’s back half, Sycamore Pass, most of Schnebly — dead zones. Tell someone your route, carry water like you mean it, and keep the radio on 106.5: it reaches where the bars don’t.

🤹 Rather ride than drive?

The local outfitters run Broken Arrow and Diamondback daily with drivers who do it a thousand times a year — worth every penny if slickrock ledges aren’t your idea of relaxing. Same rocks, zero body-panel anxiety.

The official soundtrack of doors-off
