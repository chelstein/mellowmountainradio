Background

How a mountain radio station got ahead of the networks

When most people think about a radio station’s technology, they picture a mixing board, a transmitter tower, maybe a streaming player on the website. KAZM has all of that. But it also runs something unusual for a small-market station: a live data infrastructure that’s been quietly powering one of the most feature-rich independent radio websites in the country.

Fire restriction levels from the Coconino National Forest, refreshed every six hours. Road conditions pulled from ADOT’s live feeds. Sedona weather straight from National Weather Service coordinates at the transmitter site. Concert tour dates for every artist in rotation, deduplicated and verified. A 14-day rewind archive, block by block. A song history going back to October 2023 that can answer what was playing at any minute since then.

All of that already exists. The MCP server simply opens a door into it.

The Technology

What MCP is, in plain terms

Model Context Protocol is Anthropic’s open standard for connecting AI assistants to live data and tools. Think of it as a USB port — but for knowledge. When an AI assistant needs real-world information to answer a question, it queries an MCP server. The server responds. The AI tells you, with the source intact.

“Right now, if you ask an AI ‘what are the fire restrictions in Sedona today?’ it either guesses, searches, or admits it doesn’t know. With KAZM’s MCP server, it knows.”

The protocol is model-agnostic and already supported by Claude, with integrations moving fast across the AI ecosystem. A station that has an MCP server today is positioned the way a station with a strong website was positioned in 2002 — early enough that being there matters.

What’s Exposed

17 live tools, all real data

Every tool below reads from a live source. Nothing is cached longer than the underlying data warrants. Nothing is fabricated.

get_now_playing() Current song, artist, and album from broadcast automation — the actual air signal, not a guess.
get_listener_count() How many people are tuned in right now, across all three streams.
search_song_history(date, time) What was playing at any hour since October 2023, from the station’s own broadcast logs.
get_fire_restrictions() Current Coconino National Forest fire level, refreshed every six hours from the Forest Service.
get_weather() Conditions at the Sedona transmitter site from the National Weather Service — not a stock forecast.
get_road_conditions() 89A, Oak Creek Canyon, and I-17 from ADOT live camera and incident feeds via AZ511.
get_concerts(artist?, location?) Verified, deduplicated tour dates for every artist in KAZM’s rotation — Southwest-first.
get_events() Sedona community and library events, fetched from local sources and updated daily.
get_stream_url() The direct listen-live URL — so an AI can hand someone a working link, not a homepage.
get_show_schedule() Current and upcoming on-air show lineup — who’s on, when, and what the show is about.
get_rewind(date?) 14-day broadcast archive, block by block — what ran on any hour of any recent broadcast day.
get_horoscope(sign?) Daily horoscope for all 12 signs, served from KAZM’s own data.
get_schumann_resonance() Real-time Schumann resonance readings from global monitoring stations — the Earth’s own frequency.
get_jeep_trails() Sedona-area 4x4 trails with difficulty ratings and route data — for the off-road crowd.
get_movies() What’s playing at theaters near Sedona — current showtimes from local cinema listings.
search_song_request_library(query) Search the station’s requestable song library by title or artist name.
get_emergency_alerts(severity?) Active EAS alerts for Yavapai and Coconino counties — weather emergencies, evacuations, Amber Alerts — live from NWS.
Why It Matters

Search indexes what you publish. AI queries what you make available.

These are not the same thing, and the distinction will matter more with every passing year.

A page on a website gets discovered when someone searches for it. An MCP tool gets called when an AI needs it — which is increasingly whenever anyone asks a question. The shift from “I’ll Google it” to “I’ll ask Claude” is already in progress, and most media organizations haven’t thought about what that means for their distribution.

Get connected

One line of configuration. No API key required for read tools.

To connect any MCP-compatible AI client:

Add {"mcpServers":{"kazm":{"url":"https://mcp.mellowmountainradio.com"}}} to your client’s MCP server configuration. Full tool documentation, parameters, and response schemas at mcp.mellowmountainradio.com/docs.
